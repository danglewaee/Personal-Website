I build trustworthy AI systems for decisions made under uncertainty.

I came to computer science later than many people around me, around the moment AI stopped feeling distant and started feeling useful.

What stayed with me was a harder question: how do we build systems people can trust when the stakes are real?

About

I came to computer science looking for trust.

AI opened the door. Purpose made me stay. Growing up in Vietnam, I watched what fragile forecasts and fragile systems could do, especially through my father's work in meteorology.

That question led me from river-basin data at ISTI to community work in An Giang, where I met children learning on the same old desktop computers that first pulled me toward computing myself.

The hardest lesson came early: detection is not prevention. If a system is going to help people act, it has to earn trust through data, rigor, and resilience.

Foundations

The mission is personal. The foundation has to be rigorous.

I am still early in the journey, and I do not want to sound finished before I have earned it.

What matters more is building the technical depth to stay with hard problems long enough to make something useful.

Beyond Work
