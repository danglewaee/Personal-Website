Context

This project is shaped by the kind of launch problem Meta faced with Threads: a product can grow faster than infrastructure teams can safely react. In that moment, the problem is not just scaling. It is deciding how much to change, how early, and where the real risk sits.

Why it gets hard

When growth arrives quickly, teams can neither wait until traffic is already spiking nor overreact by spraying excess capacity everywhere. The real difficulty is balancing speed, cost, and reliability at the same time.

What most systems still miss

Autoscaling alone reacts after the fact. It does not tell operators what is likely to happen next, what the safest capacity plan is, or how to roll out that change without turning one bad decision into a larger reliability event.

What I built

I built a decision layer for cloud infrastructure that combines workload forecasting, optimization, and rollout guardrails so teams can compare scenarios, choose a plan earlier, and scale more deliberately instead of reactively.

Why teams can trust it

The system is built to be trusted before action, not after regret. It benchmarks against reactive baselines, tests decisions in shadow mode, and wraps changes in canary and rollback logic so optimization does not come at the cost of operational recklessness.

Mission link

It carries the same mission into cloud infrastructure: turning uncertain tradeoffs into decisions teams can trust enough to act on.
