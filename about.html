My journey

AI first drew me into computer science, but forecasting, trust, and the realities of growing up in Vietnam gave that interest a direction.

This page is the fuller story behind the systems I choose to build and the kind of work I want to keep doing.

Where it started

AI opened the door. Forecasting made it personal.

I came to computer science later than many people around me, at the moment AI stopped feeling distant and started to feel like it could reshape entire industries.

What made me stay was more personal. Growing up in Vietnam, I watched how much people had to rely on forecasts and public systems that were still imperfect, especially through my father's work in meteorology.

What An Giang taught me

Useful systems have to earn trust.

At ISTI and later through community work in An Giang, I moved from fascination into building. The water-monitoring system I self-funded could detect problems, but that experience taught me a harder lesson: detection is not prevention.

If a system is going to help people act, it needs more than technical novelty. It needs better data, stronger evidence, and enough trust for people to actually rely on it.

What stays with me

Signal, patience, and staying calm under pressure.

That instinct shows up outside engineering too, from detective fiction and codebreaking stories to the patience I learned from following Manchester United through long rebuilding years.

I keep returning to the same habit of mind: when the first explanation fails, slow down, look again, and keep searching for the signal that matters.
