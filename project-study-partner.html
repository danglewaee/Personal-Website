Who it helps

Built for students whose weeks are packed with classes, deadlines, and overlapping tasks, but who still need a plan they can realistically follow.

What makes the moment hard

The hard part is not knowing that the workload is heavy. It is knowing what to do first, how much to take on, and how to avoid building a schedule that collapses halfway through the week.

What the system changes

The system turns messy task dumps into more realistic weekly plans by accounting for deadlines, workload, sleep, and recovery, then replanning when disruptions happen.

Why it earns trust

Trust comes from the fact that it does not just produce an optimized schedule. It is designed to keep plans realistic, preserve as much of the schedule as possible when things change, and benchmark against simpler baselines instead of assuming the first plan is always good enough.

Why it belongs here

It brings the same mission down to human scale: turning messy constraints into decisions people can trust enough to act on every day.
